1 · Workcenters

Workcenters are arranged left → right on the factory floor in this order. Each has parallel capacity, a default processing-time distribution (used when adding routing steps), and optional breakdowns.

2 · Parts & BOM

Purchased parts come from raw supply — no operations needed. Produced parts consume their BOM components to start production, then follow their routing. Mark finished products with customer demand to show them exiting the floor.

3 · Routings

The routing is the ordered sequence of operations a produced part goes through after its BOM components are available. Each operation runs at a workcenter with its own processing-time distribution.

4 · Factory floor

Colored lines are part routings flowing left → right through workcenter columns. ⊕ marks a BOM assembly point where component flows merge into a product's flow. Lines converging on the same column share that workcenter's capacity.
