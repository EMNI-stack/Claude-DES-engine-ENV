Discrete-event simulation · teaching tool

Learn to model, run, and judge a factory line — not just watch one.

A browser-based environment for the course Simulering og produktionslayout. The factory floor is the worked domain; the real subject is the discipline of simulation — stating assumptions, modelling inputs, verifying and validating, and reading results with the uncertainty kept in view.

What this tool is

A method, made visible

The simulation study process is the backbone of the interface, following Robinson — conceptual model, assumptions, input data, V&V, and output analysis are steps you move through, not afterthoughts.

Layout meets flow

Place resources on a 2D floor; distance sets transport delay, so where things sit changes how the line performs. Simple by design — placement, distance, limited movers.

Theory you can test against

Compare simulated output to Factory Physics references — Little's Law, the VUT curve, best/worst/practical-worst-case — to see where the formulas hold and where simulation earns its keep.

Foundation

Built on a validated engine

This application reuses the existing, test-covered discrete-event engine (event loop, blocking, breakdowns, scrap, push/pull, BOM/routing) rather than re-implementing it. The engine check page drives that engine from this new app to prove the wiring. The original prototype remains available as reference: production line · factory builder.
